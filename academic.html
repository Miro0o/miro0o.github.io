About

I am an MSc student in Computer Science and Engineering at DTU. My current interests lie at the intersection of software analysis and large language models. I am especially interested in LLM-based methods, including agents, that complement traditional techniques to improve software security, correctness, reliability, and efficiency.

More broadly, I think of language as any system for representing information or the world—from natural languages and formal notation to programs and learned representations. This view connects my interests in mathematics, logic, formal methods, programming languages and program analysis, natural language processing, and generative and predictive AI. I also enjoy learning history, philosophy, and foreign languages (Chinese is my native language), which give me another perspective on how language supports thought, computation, and intelligence. To me, these subjects share a deeper connection: the possibility of understanding computation /intelligence through language.

Before coming to DTU, I studied cybersecurity at Sichuan University and spent a semester as a visiting student at UC Berkeley. I have experience working with network and cloud technologies (eBPF, DPDK, SDN, etc.) and security techniques (penetration testing, malware analysis, fuzzing, etc.).
